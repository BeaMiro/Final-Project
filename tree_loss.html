    Tree Loss Over the Years

    The loss of trees greatly affects organisms of all types, especially the plants and animals that depend on them. By cutting down trees, many organisms have lost their habitats and therefore become endangered. In the data visualizations below, the amount of tree loss in each of the 12 most populated states is shown from the year 2014 all the way till 2024. By examining tree loss over the course of a decade, we can better identify which states have had major habitat loss for its organisms. We can see that California experiences the highest tree loss of all states, with a sharp increase around 2020 and 2021. This may be attributed to the severe wildfires occured in 2020. Meanwhile, Illinois and New Jersey consistently show small levels of tree loss across the years. Apart from California, Georgia indicates a sustained tree loss over the years. However, it reached a peak in 2017. Research indicates that during this year, a massive wildfire caused by lightning burned over 145,000 in the Okefenokee Swamp area of Georgia. This shows how other factors can affect the habitats that organisms depend on. Therefore, the states with the most amount of tree loss overall are California and Georgia- this shows that tree loss is a major threat to endangered animals in these two states.
